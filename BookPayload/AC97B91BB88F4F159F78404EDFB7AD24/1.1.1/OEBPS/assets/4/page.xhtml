By the 18th century Poland was weak. This gave Russia, Prussia and Austria an excuse to split it between them.

Danzig became part of Prussia and its German outlook was an advantage. Unlike the rest of ‘West Prussia’, it did not need to be ‘Germanised’.

Towards the end of the First World War Britain and her allies agreed to recreate Poland. They knew this meant that the German city of Danzig would be cut off from the rest of Germany.

The Treaty of Versailles made Danzig into a 'Free City'. Poland could use the port, but Danzig itself would not be part of Poland. A democratic government was set up in the city. The League of Nations sent a representative to make sure that Danzig was properly run.
