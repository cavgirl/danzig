The Treaty of Versailles created tensions between Poland and Germany over Danzig.

During the the 1930s Hitler's Germany demanded the return of Danzig. Poland refused. However, most Germans living inside Danzig also wished to rejoin Germany. They voted for the Nazis and this gave Hitler the excuse he needed.

Germany invaded Poland in September 1939. This was the start of the Second World War.

By 1945, the old city of Danzig lay in ruins.
