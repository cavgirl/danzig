Gdansk was taken over by the German Teutonic Knights in the 14th century. They brought in Germans and drove out the Poles.

In 1454 the city became part of Poland again, but the population stayed German. This caused friction with the King of Poland, but Danzig's growing wealth meant he permitted the city to keep its special privileges.

...

 

 

In 1743 the Danzig Research Society was founded. It was the oldest German language research society in Europe. Yet the population of Danzig still thought of themselves as Polish.

The Polish port of Gdansk was particularly vulnerable. A port was necessary for trade. Trade meant wealth.

 

As a result, countries would fight for access to a good port. This meant Gdansk changed hands frequently. It was German as often as it was Polish.
